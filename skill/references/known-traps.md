# Known Verification Traps

Programs that have specifically caused errors in past newsletters, with notes on what to watch for. **Read this file before finalising any draft.**

---

## Freedom Week (ASI/IEA)

- **The trap**: The freedomweek.org page text has shifted multiple times mid-cycle — sometimes "Applications open", sometimes "Applications closed", sometimes with a specific deadline, sometimes rolling.
- **Verification rule**: Always **direct fetch** https://freedomweek.org/apply (not the ASI homepage, which is often months out of date). Do not trust search snippets or older sources. If the live `/apply` page says closed, drop it.

## Beloff Conference (Vinson Centre)

- **The trap**: Dates have changed mid-cycle (e.g. previously listed as 14–16 September, later updated to 7–9 September). At points the page only says "taking place in Autumn... details to come closer to the date" — meaning applications had not yet opened despite previous drafts listing dates.
- **Verification rule**: Only list Beloff if the live https://vinsoncentre.com/programmes/beloff-conference/ page shows a specific application channel (e.g. email contact or form). If dates are TBC, drop the listing.

## IEA Future Thought Leaders' Programme (Undergraduate)

- **The trap**: Easy to confuse with the **Sixth Form** version, which has different dates and a different deadline. Sam's audience is the undergraduate version.
- **Verification rule**: Only fetch https://iea.org.uk/students/future-thought-leaders-programme-undergraduate/ for this listing. Page sometimes lists wave dates without a deadline — when this happens, use "Apply early" rather than guess.

## IEA General Internship

- **The trap**: Three intakes per year (Spring, Summer, Autumn) with separate deadlines. The page often still shows a just-passed deadline prominently.
- **Verification rule**: Confirm each listed deadline is after today. List only the still-open intake(s).

## CPS Research Internship

- **The trap**: The CPS's own vacancies page at https://cps.org.uk/about/vacancies/ frequently reads "not currently hiring" even when a Research Intern listing is live on w4mp.
- **Verification rule**: If cps.org.uk shows no listings, check **w4mpjobs.org** for an active CPS Research Intern posting. The w4mp listing is authoritative when the CPS page is blank.

## AIER Seminars

- **The trap**: AIER runs multiple seminars per year at partner campuses, each with its own deadline. Past newsletters have listed seminars whose deadlines had already passed.
- **Verification rule**: For each seminar listed, check the specific seminar page (not the overview) and confirm the deadline is in the future. AIER pages sometimes throw 415 errors via direct fetch — use web search as fallback to find each seminar's deadline.

## Mannkal Scholarship Program

- **The trap**: Page often still references prior year's eligibility ("2025") into the new calendar year. Restricted to Western Australian undergraduates with AU citizenship/PR.
- **Verification rule**: Don't list unless Sam confirms readership includes WA students AND the live page confirms an open window.

## Vinson Hayek Internship

- **The trap**: Three cycles per year (Autumn end of June, Winter end of October, Spring end of January). Easy to list the wrong cycle's deadline.
- **Verification rule**: When the live page doesn't show a precise deadline for the next cycle, use "Contact VinsonCentre@buckingham.ac.uk for the current application window".

## Cato, Heritage, AIER (US programs)

- **The trap**: Each runs Spring, Summer, and Fall cohorts. Listing the wrong cohort is a recurring error.
- **Verification rule**: Confirm which cohort is currently accepting applications and list only that one. Don't pre-list a future cohort that hasn't yet opened.

## IEA FTL Sixth Form vs. Undergraduate

- **The trap**: Two separate IEA Future Thought Leaders programmes at different URLs — the Sixth Form one is for school students, the Undergraduate one is for university students. Sam's audience is the Undergraduate version.

## AEI's two Honors tracks

- **The trap**: AEI runs both a **Summer Honors Program** (1-week residential seminars) and a **Summer Honors Academy** (9-week residential program with full-time internship). Different applications, different deadlines, different commitments. Don't conflate.

## Discontinued / not-active

- **IHS (Institute for Humane Studies)** — earlier "discontinued" assessment of undergraduate Summer Seminars may have been overstated. Current programming has shifted heavily toward graduate/postdoc, but undergraduate seminars, grants, and the Hayek Fund for Scholars may still run. **Always verify current undergraduate programs on theihs.org directly** before listing — search returns can be misleading.
- **Mercatus Summer Internship** — deadlines typically March/April and very rigid. Verify each year.
- **Atlas Liberty Forums** — these are paid conferences, not student programs. Only list if there's a specific student scholarship for an upcoming forum.

---

## General verification protocol

1. **Direct fetch** the specific program page, not the org homepage and not a search engine snippet.
2. If the page won't fetch, do a targeted web search with the org name, program name, and current year — but treat search snippets with skepticism, especially for fast-moving programs.
3. **Today's date matters**: confirm any listed deadline is after today.
4. When uncertain, **omit the program** rather than risk listing a closed one.
5. The spreadsheet attachment follows the same rule — closed programs are excluded.
